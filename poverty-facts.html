What is Poverty?

Poverty is defined as the state of one's living conditions based on their financial position. Many are considered to be living in poverty due to various social, economic, and political factors. Internationally, poverty is often measured by daily income. For example, individuals living on less than $2.15 per day (or even $1.90, depending on the measure) are classified as living in extreme poverty.

In Wales, research such as the 2016-17 National Survey for Wales indicated that 1% of households had received food from a food bank in the last 12 months, hinting at local challenges that echo global trends.

Why and How Does Poverty Occur?

Why?

Although poverty does not have a single cause, several factors contribute to it. Commonly, these include rising living costs (food, rent, bills, etc.), low wages, and a lack of job opportunities—especially for specific groups. These factors create a cycle that perpetuates poverty both locally and globally.

How?

Today, poverty is addressed in various ways including charitable efforts, community awareness campaigns, and social initiatives. However, despite these efforts, poverty rates in many places remain high or are even growing. By increasing awareness and instituting targeted measures (such as international laws), there is hope for significant reduction in poverty levels.

Rationale

The project is built on three main reasons, focusing on how reducing poverty can have a ripple effect in improving economic stability, enhancing public services, and reducing other major social issues. By addressing poverty, we open the door to higher consumer demand, business growth, increased tax revenue, and overall societal well-being.

PESTLE Analysis

Political

Providing people with their basic needs can reduce violence and conflicts within communities.

Economic

Reducing poverty increases disposable income, boosts consumer demand, and expands the middle class—ultimately driving economic growth.

Social

Improved economic conditions lead to better healthcare, increased participation in social activities, and overall higher quality of life.

Technological

A reduction in poverty means more individuals can participate as innovators, creators, and consumers, accelerating technological advancements.

Legal

With more resources available, legal systems improve and crime rates can decrease, leading to a more secure society.

Environmental

Reducing poverty can lessen the reliance on harmful practices such as illegal wildlife trafficking and promote access to clean energy.

Articles and Research Findings

Below are some key articles and findings from various sources:

  • World Bank: Efforts over 30 years reduced global extreme poverty, though the COVID-19 pandemic increased numbers by 70 million.
    Key Facts: Extreme poverty rate increased to 9.3% in 2019; up to 685 million people may still be in extreme poverty by the end of 2022.
  • Compassion: Nearly one in ten people worldwide live below the international poverty line, with 689 million surviving on less than $1.90 a day.
  • Brookings (Bookings): Significant progress was seen in 2015 with 728 million people living in poverty, though challenges remain in Africa.
  • World Vision: 9.2% of the global population lives on less than $2.15 a day, with a high concentration of extreme poverty in sub-Saharan Africa.
  • The Guardian: Since 1981, about 1 billion people have escaped extreme poverty, though 9.1% of the global population was estimated to be in poverty in 2016.
  • United Nations Global Compact: Approximately 10% of the global population lives in extreme poverty, influenced by conflicts, climate change, and COVID-19.
  • DoSomething.org: Highlights that poverty is multifaceted—beyond income, it includes access to healthcare, education, and housing.
  • Public Health (Northern Ireland): Economic inequality significantly impacts health with higher poverty rates noted in Northern Ireland compared to Great Britain.

Abstract

Poverty is not an unsolvable problem but an option—a result of choices, policies, and systemic issues like greed and corruption. Ending poverty could initiate a domino effect, potentially addressing many other major global challenges.
